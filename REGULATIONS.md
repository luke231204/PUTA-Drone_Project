# OTBAN Wilayah VI Padang — Drone Operations Regulatory Guide

This document provides a consolidated, official reference guide for the operational and administrative regulations governing **Unmanned Aircraft Systems (UAS / PUTA - Pesawat Udara Tanpa Awak)** in Indonesia, specifically within the jurisdiction of **Kantor Otoritas Bandar Udara Wilayah VI Padang**.

These regulations form the legal framework for the airspace compliance checks and telemetry audits performed by **PUTA-Monitor**.

---

## 1. Quick Reference: Operational Limits

| Parameter | Standard Limit | Regulation Reference | Notes |

| **Max Altitude** | **400 feet** (120 meters) AGL | PM 63/2021 & PM 37/2020 | Above Ground Level (unless near a building). |

| **Accident Reporting** | Max **10 calendar days** | PM 63/2021 (CASR 107.9) | Report to DGCA/OTBAN if serious injury or damage. |

## 2. PM 37 Tahun 2020 — Airspace & Safety Buffers

This regulation defines where and how drones can be operated relative to civil aviation infrastructure and various classes of airspace in Indonesia.

### 2.1 Airspace Classifications
*   **Controlled Airspace:** Berapapun ketinggiannya (at any altitude), drone operations **must** obtain official approval from the Directorate General of Civil Aviation (DGCA / DJPU) and coordinate with AirNav.
*   **Uncontrolled Airspace:**
    *   **$\le$ 400 feet (120 meters) AGL:** No prior DGCA approval required.
    *   **> 400 feet (120 meters) AGL:** DGCA approval is **mandatory**.

### 2.2 Safety Buffer Corridors
*   **Kawasan Keselamatan Operasi Penerbangan (KKOP):** Drones are strictly prohibited within the 5 km lateral buffer zone of all airports unless prior coordination, safety assessment, and explicit approval are obtained.
*   **Helipads:** Drones operated within a **3 Nautical Miles (NM)** radius of a helipad outside KKOP boundaries require prior approval.
*   **Prohibited & Restricted Areas:** Drones must maintain a minimum distance of **500 meters** from the outer boundaries of any military, government, or state prohibited/restricted airspace.
*   **Populated Areas:** Flying directly over crowds, public highways, or residential settlements is prohibited unless specifically authorized.

### 2.3 Permit Coordination Timeline
*   **New Approvals:** Applications must be submitted to the DGCA at least **14 working days** before the scheduled flight.
*   **Schedule Changes:** Flight plan adjustments must be submitted at least **7 working days** prior to the flight.
*   **AirNav Notification:** Operator must notify the local AirNav tower at least **24 hours** before takeoff.

## 3. PM 63 Tahun 2021 (CASR Part 107) — Small UAS Rules

This regulation adopts the Civil Aviation Safety Regulations (CASR) Part 107, governing small drones ($\le$ 25 kg) used for commercial, government, and specialized civil purposes.

### 3.1 Operating Constraints
*   **Maximum Speed:** **87 knots** (100 mph or 160 km/h).
*   **Maximum Altitude:** **400 feet** (120 meters) AGL.
*   **In-flight Visibility:** Visual line of sight (VLOS) must be maintained. Horizontal visibility from the ground control station must be at least **3 miles (4.8 km)**.
*   **Cloud Clearance:**
    *   Must remain at least **500 feet (150 m) below** clouds.
    *   Must remain at least **2,000 feet (600 m) horizontally** away from clouds.
*   **Single-UAS Rule:** A pilot is strictly prohibited from operating more than one drone at the same time.

### 3.2 Remote Pilot Certification (RPIC)
*   To operate a drone for civil or commercial tasks in Indonesia, the pilot must hold a **Remote Pilot Certificate** with a Small UAS rating.
*   **Requirements:**
    *   Minimum age of **17 years**.
    *   Proficient in reading, writing, and speaking English.
    *   Must be physically and mentally fit.
    *   Pass the initial DGCA aeronautical knowledge exam.
    *   **Currency Check:** Pilot credentials must be refreshed/re-evaluated every **24 calendar months**.

### 3.3 Incident & Accident Reporting
If a drone operation results in a serious accident, the Remote Pilot in Command (RPIC) must submit an official report to the DGCA/OTBAN within **10 calendar days**. Reportable accidents include:
*   Serious injury to any person.
*   Loss of consciousness of any person.
*   Damage to property (other than the drone) where repair costs exceed the regulatory threshold.

## 4. KP 242 Tahun 2019 — Registration & Markings

This Decree of the Director General of Civil Aviation defines the registration requirements and physical marking specifications for all small unmanned aircraft.

### 4.1 PUTA Registration Rules
*   **Applicability:** Applies to all drones with a gross takeoff weight (including battery and payload) **greater than 250 grams** and **less than 25 kg**.
*   **Eligibility:** Must be owned by an Indonesian citizen (WNI) or an Indonesian legal entity.
*   **Double Registration:** A drone cannot be registered in Indonesia if it is currently registered in another country.
*   **Validity:** The registration certificate is valid for **3 years** and must be renewed online via IMSIS DJPU.

### 4.2 Physical Identification Markings
Upon registration, the drone is assigned an 8-character registration mark (combination of letters and numbers). It must be displayed as follows:
*   **Placement:** Must be permanently painted or affixed to the **under-surface** (bottom) and/or **both sides** of the fuselage.
*   **Height Sizing:**
    *   **Bottom Surface:** Minimal **5 cm** height.
    *   **Side Fuselage:** Minimal **3 cm** height.
*   **Dimensions:**
    *   Width of characters must be $2/3$ of the height (except number '1' and letter 'I').
    *   Line thickness and spacing must be $1/6$ of the height.

## 5. Audit Compliance Checklist

When performing audits using **PUTA-Monitor**, ensure the following documentation is uploaded and verified:

1.  [ ] **Drone Registration Mark:** Check physical identification on fuselage matches the registration certificate.
2.  [ ] **Remote Pilot Certificate:** Verify pilot license is active and currency is within the 24-month limit.
3.  [ ] **Insurance Policy:** Verify valid third-party liability insurance is active.
4.  [ ] **Flight Permit (DJPU Approval):** Check coordinates, altitude, and date bounds.
5.  [ ] **AirNav Coordination:** Verify ATC tower log confirms flight plan submission (24 hours prior).
6.  [ ] **Telemetry Log Audits:** Validate that maximum speed did not exceed 87 knots, altitude did not exceed the permit ceiling, and operations were confined to daylight hours within the approved coordinate bounds.
